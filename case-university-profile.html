Case study · University

Raising research profile & reputation

How a university with excellent research but a quiet voice built a sharper research identity — and a public profile to match the quality of its work.

BR
BuildResearch
Research & Thought Leadership
University · Positioning · Thought leadership

The setting

A university department was producing genuinely strong research — well-regarded by peers, solidly published, methodologically sound. Outside the academy, almost nobody knew. Its public profile didn't reflect the quality of its work: media enquiries went elsewhere, policy teams cited competitors, and prospective partners struggled to say what the department stood for.

The problem wasn't the research. It was identity and translation — no clear answer to "known for what?", and no bridge between the journal and the public conversation.

What we did

We worked with the department to build both the identity and the machinery to express it:

  • Research positioning — defining the two or three themes the department could credibly own.
  • A thought-leadership rhythm — turning ongoing research into a steady drumbeat of accessible, citable output.
  • Translation craft — training researchers to write and speak for audiences beyond peer review.
  • Profile infrastructure — the formats, channels and cadence to keep the voice consistent.
Reputation follows clarity. Once the department could say what it stood for, every piece of work started compounding into profile.

What changed

The department's research didn't change — the world's ability to find, understand and cite it did.

Outcomes

  • A sharper research identity — clearly owned themes and a consistent voice
  • A stronger external profile with policy, media and partner audiences
  • Researchers confident writing and speaking beyond the academy

This case is illustrative of the work we do; details are generalised to respect client confidentiality.
