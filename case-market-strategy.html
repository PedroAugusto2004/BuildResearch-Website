Evidence to power a market strategy

How a commercial organisation replaced instinct with evidence — building a market study that sharpened its proposition and gave its strategy a defensible foundation.

BR
BuildResearch
Research & Thought Leadership
Commercial · Strategy · Market study

The setting

A commercial organisation was preparing a significant strategic move: repositioning its core proposition in a market it believed was shifting under its feet. The leadership team had conviction and anecdote in abundance — client conversations, sales signals, competitor moves — but no systematic evidence to test the thesis, size the opportunity or persuade a sceptical board.

The risk was real: committing investment to a strategy built on the loudest voices in the room rather than the state of the market.

What we did

We designed a market study around the decisions the organisation actually had to make — not a general survey of everything. That meant:

  • Framing the real questions — turning a broad strategic thesis into testable, decision-relevant hypotheses.
  • Mixed methods — quantitative demand evidence, paired with depth interviews across buyers and non-buyers.
  • Competitive context — where the proposition was genuinely distinctive, and where it only felt that way.
  • Decision-ready storytelling — findings structured around the choices on the table, designed for the boardroom.
The study didn't just describe the market — it told the leadership which of their assumptions survived contact with the evidence, and which didn't.

What changed

Some assumptions held; others didn't survive. Both results were valuable — the strategy that emerged was sharper, more focused and easier to defend.

Outcomes

  • Market-defining insight that sharpened the proposition
  • A strategy the board could interrogate — and back — on evidence
  • A repeatable research approach for the next strategic question

This case is illustrative of the work we do; details are generalised to respect client confidentiality.
