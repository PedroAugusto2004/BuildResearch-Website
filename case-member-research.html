Case study · Professional body

Setting a new standard for member research

How a professional body moved from occasional, ad-hoc member surveys to a benchmark research programme its members now rely on — and cite.

BR
BuildResearch
Research & Thought Leadership
Professional body · Standards · Flagship report

The setting

A long-established professional body had something most organisations envy: privileged access to thousands of members with real expertise and strong opinions. What it lacked was a way to turn that access into authority. Member research happened in pockets — different teams, different methods, different question banks — and the results rarely travelled beyond the team that commissioned them.

The consequence was familiar: no single, citable view of the profession, inconsistent quality, and a growing sense that the body was speaking about its members rather than for them.

What we did

We started by listening — reviewing every recent study, interviewing the teams behind them, and mapping where evidence was strong, duplicated or missing. From there we worked with the body to design a single programme architecture:

  • A common standard — shared methods, question banks and quality criteria so every study builds on the last.
  • A flagship annual study — one rigorous, well-designed report that anchors the year and earns citations.
  • A clear editorial voice — findings written and designed to be used, quoted and remembered.
  • Capability transfer — coaching the in-house team to run the programme themselves.
The goal was never a report. It was a programme — one the members would recognise as the definitive account of their profession.

What changed

The shift was structural, not cosmetic. Research moved from a scattered activity to a named, expected programme with a standard its members can see.

Outcomes

  • A benchmark research programme its members now rely on and cite
  • Shared standards and question banks that raise quality with every wave
  • An in-house team confident to design, run and publish the programme

This case is illustrative of the work we do; details are generalised to respect client confidentiality.
